Bear

  1. Ollie
  2. Mona

A bear is a large mammal characterized by a stocky build, thick fur (which can vary in color from black, brown, to white), and a short tail. They are omnivorous, feeding on a wide range of foods including berries, fish, insects, and occasionally small mammals. Bears are known for their strength and are often found in forests and mountainous regions across the world.

Giraffes

  1. Frankie
  2. Coconut

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, known for their long necks and distinctive spotted coat patterns. They have long legs, ossicones (horn-like structures), and a prehensile tongue that helps them grasp leaves from tall trees. Giraffes inhabit savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands in sub-Saharan Africa, where they primarily feed on leaves and buds from acacia trees.

Lions

  1. Mella
  2. Karl

Lions are large carnivorous cats known for their muscular build, golden fur (males) or tawny fur (females), and prominent mane (in males). They are apex predators and live in social groups called prides, typically found in savannas and grasslands of Africa. Lions hunt cooperatively, primarily preying on large herbivores like zebras and buffalo.

Monkeys
  1. Cookie
  2. Earl
  3. Banna Pudding

Monkeys are primates characterized by their agile bodies, long tails (though some species are tailless), and opposable thumbs. They are highly intelligent and social animals, living in diverse habitats from tropical forests to deserts. Monkeys have varied diets including fruits, insects, and leaves, and they exhibit a wide range of behaviors such as grooming, playfulness, and complex social hierarchies.

Alligators
  1. Wren
  2. Aspen
  3. Mika

Alligators are large reptiles with a broad, rounded snout and a muscular body covered in armored scales. They are semiaquatic, inhabiting freshwater environments like swamps, marshes, and rivers across the southeastern United States and China. Alligators are carnivorous predators, feeding on fish, birds, mammals, and occasionally smaller alligators, and they are known for their powerful jaws and ability to move quickly both on land and in water.
